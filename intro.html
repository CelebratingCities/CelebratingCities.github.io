What's this hackathon about?

DOT continues to help Americans reach opportunity by ensuring that America’s transportation system provides reliable, safe, and affordable ways for people to reach jobs, education, and other essential services outside their communities.

Uber creates possibilities for riders, drivers, and cities. For the women and men who drive with Uber, the Uber app represents a flexible new way to earn money. For cities, Uber helps strengthen local economies, improves access to transportation, and makes streets safer. We believe that when transportation is as reliable as running water, everyone benefits.

Transportation projects can ensure reliable, safe, and affordable access to jobs, education, and other essential services. But that isn’t happening for everyone. For example, a New York University Rudin Center for Transportation study compared neighborhoods by accessibility to mass transit and the number of jobs within an hour’s commute. It found that areas with some, but insufficient, access to transportation — had the highest rates of unemployment and the lowest incomes. On the other hand, residents of areas well-served by transit or who could rely on personal vehicles had reasonable access to opportunity.

The theme of this hackathon is therefore: Celebrating Cities: Connecting the People of Baltimore, Maryland, and DC with Opportunity through Transportation.

Problem Statements

Catalyze neighborhood revitalization in underserved urban areas

Focus on the area near the West Baltimore MARC Station, where existing transportation access provides a good starting point for neighborhood revitalization. Using Federal data sets in combination with local data sets, show how the West Baltimore MARC station provides access to jobs, job training, quality education, and other services. Describe relevant demographics like unemployment, income level, and car ownership in the surrounding neighborhood. Using the Uber Development Platform, demonstrate how accessing Uber services might increase the connectivity of this area. Compare the cost and time profiles for use of Uber services in combination with transit against the Location Affordability Index for the same area.

Tom's Vignette
  • Background: Tom is a community advocate who wants to encourage investment in new transportation options in underserved areas.
  • Challenge: Help Tom determine which areas of Baltimore have high unemployment, low car ownership, and poor transit access.
  • Potential Datasets:
    • City SDK
    • Baltimore Transit Datasets
Michelle's Vignette
  • Background: Michelle is a senior citizen on a fixed income. She wants to remain in the DC area to stay close to family, but recent increases in housing costs in the area have made this challenging. She needs to find a safe area to live while maintaining access to health care providers.
  • Challenge: Help Michelle narrow down her choice of neighborhoods using access to health care facilities and crime rates.
  • Potential Datasets:
    • Location Affordabilty Index
    • DC Transportation Data
    • DC Primary Care Facilities
    • DC Crime Data

Serving last-mile mobility

Public transit and other services provide numerous mobility options for citizens. However, it can be difficult to obtain convenient, affordable and safe access to these services in underserved areas. Using the Uber Developer Platform, develop compelling applications with easy-to-use features, services, and tools that can help bridge that gap. Incorporate non-transportation information, like public safety (crime) and access to quality schools.

Janice Vignette
  • Background: Janice doesn’t own a car, and she’s determined to keep her transportation budget reasonable. She wants to move out of the city to an opportunity area with low crime and high-performing schools, but also needs to keep her commute time as low as possible.
  • Challenge: Build a tool that helps Janice balance her transportation costs and time spent commuting, focusing on affordable housing in opportunity areas.
  • Potential Datasets:
    • Location Affordability Index
    • 2012 CHAS
    • HUD Affordable Housing
    • Housing Choice Vouchers
    • School Proficiency Index
    • Jobs Index
    • Baltimore and DC Transit Datasets
John's Vignette
  • Background: John is a single father of two children, aged 6 and 3, who needs to find housing in Baltimore. When evaluating potential neighborhoods, he needs to balance affordability, and access to employment opportunities and education for his children (both Head Start and elementary schools).
  • Challenge: Build a tool that allows John to evaluate neighborhoods on cost, access to employment centers, and access to education.
  • Potential Datasets:
    • Location Affordability Index
    • Head Start Centers
    • Department of Education Data Inventory
    • Jobs Proximity Index

Visualizing Time Use for Different Populations

When it comes to transportation access, inequality is more than just a question of affordability — it’s also a question of time. Using Federal data sets about time use, visualize time use disparities for differing ethnic groups and income levels. Connect these data sets with Federal data sets about ethnic and demographic profiles to show which communities might benefit from increased transportation options.

Santiago Vignette
  • Background: Santiago is a data scientist who is volunteering with his local civic hacking group to better understand inequality and access to opportunity. He has access to the Department of Labor's time use survey (by metropolitan area) but wants to better communicate the impact of time use disparities at a neighborhood level.
  • Challenge: Help Santiago build a tool to better understand the differences in time use among ethnic groups and income levels, and help him show which communities in his neighborhood in Baltimore might benefit from increased transportation options.
  • Potential Datasets:
    • Department of Labor Time-use Survey
    • City SDK

About the Event

Uber is collaborating with the U.S. Department of Transportation (DOT) to help develop creative ladders of opportunity in the Baltimore - DC metropolitan area. Our mission is to spur creative solutions to last-mile problems and enable fast, safe, efficient, accessible and convenient transportation options that enhance the quality of life of the American people, today and into the future.
